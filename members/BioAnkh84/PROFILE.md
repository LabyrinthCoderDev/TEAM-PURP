## About

Independent developer building Echo Root — a governed habitat for human-AI collaboration.

Started with a question about execution governance and kept building:
What does it look like when a system can observe itself, remember itself, replay itself, and govern itself?

Not just an AI assistant. A consent-gated, evidence-driven operational environment.

Public material for Echo Root focuses on the visible structure:

```text
human request
→ consent and scope
→ governance gate
→ enforcement
→ execution
→ receipt
→ observer
```

The private habitat work stays private. Team PURP gets the public shape, status, and boundaries.

## What Echo Root Does Today

- separates decision authority from execution
- records governed actions as receipts
- treats unclear requests as review moments instead of pretending certainty
- keeps local AI operation bounded by consent and scope
- supports operator-facing visibility through HUD, observer, and ledger concepts

---

## What Echo Root Does Not Do

- it does not grant AI unlimited autonomy
- it does not replace human approval
- it does not expose private memories, private ledgers, or sensitive habitat data
- it does not claim general intelligence
- it does not treat confidence as permission
